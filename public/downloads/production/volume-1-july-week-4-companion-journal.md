# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## July Week 4: Trusting Love in Hard Seasons

Use these pages with Days 203-209.

### Week Prayer

Father, teach me to trust Your promise in named places, contested places, long waiting, Sabbath rest, covenant renewal, reverent surrender, and future fruitfulness. Let Your love form obedience without performance. Amen.

### Day 203 Reflection

What detail has God already made clear while I am still waiting for fuller possession?

Practice: Write one clear detail and obey that detail without demanding the whole inheritance today.

### Day 204 Reflection

What difficult place needs to be named honestly before God instead of left vague in fear?

Practice: Name the place, then take one obedient step that agrees with God's authority there.

### Day 205 Reflection

What concern has felt endless, and how might God be asking me to see it as bounded under His care?

Practice: Write the concern as a bounded phrase, pray over it, and choose the next faithful action.

### Day 206 Sabbath Reflection

Where has long waiting made me forget that God is still strong enough to sustain His promise?

Practice: On this seventh-day Sabbath, rest one weary place under God's revealed strength before taking action.

### Day 207 Reflection

What expectation has fear made smaller than God's covenant promise?

Practice: Ask God to refine one small expectation under His "I will."

### Day 208 Reflection

Where is God inviting me to bow in trust instead of standing guarded in control?

Practice: Begin prayer in a quiet posture of reverence and surrender one defensive place.

### Day 209 Reflection

What small act of mercy could become seed for fruitfulness I cannot yet see?

Practice: Choose one small act of covenant mercy that plants toward a future you cannot yet measure.

### Week Review

- What clear detail did I obey while waiting?
- What difficult place did I name before God?
- How did Sabbath rest renew trust in God's strength?
- What small expectation did covenant mercy refine?
- What small act of mercy became seed for future fruitfulness?

# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## March Week 3: The Way in Ordinary Days Through Surrendered Love and Truth

Use these pages with Days 074-080.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, heal my grasping with surrender, make accountability honest and peaceful, form simple obedience in me, gather my divided love back to God, enlarge my confession of who You are, save me from a too-small view of Christ, and let Sabbath rest make me teachable under Your truth. Amen.

### Day 074 Reflection

Where has control replaced gratitude in the way I hold what God has entrusted to me?

Practice: Name one place you are gripping control, then pray a simple surrender over it before acting.

### Day 075 Reflection

What area of stewardship needs honest review with Jesus today?

Practice: Review one area of stewardship honestly with Jesus, then make one corrective choice.

### Day 076 Reflection

What clear step has Jesus already shown me that I need to stop delaying?

Practice: Identify one clear act of obedience and complete it without unnecessary delay.

### Day 077 Reflection

Where is my love divided, and how is Jesus calling it back to God?

Practice: Before one decision, ask, "What response would help me love God with more wholeness?"

### Day 078 Reflection

Where does my life need to agree more deeply with who Jesus truly is?

Practice: Write one sentence confessing who Jesus is, then name one ordinary choice that should align with that confession.

### Day 079 Reflection

Where have I been living with too small a view of Jesus?

Practice: Name one problem that feels large, then confess Jesus as Lord over it before you respond.

### Day 080 Sabbath Reflection

Where is Jesus inviting my public words and private life into deeper agreement?

Practice: On this Sabbath, ask Jesus to show one place where your public words and private life need more integrity.

### Week Review

- Where did surrender replace grasping?
- What accountability became healing instead of fear?
- What simple obedience did I stop delaying?
- How did love for God order an ordinary decision?
- How did confession of Jesus shape one choice?
- Where did Jesus become larger than a problem?
- How did Sabbath rest make correction feel like mercy?

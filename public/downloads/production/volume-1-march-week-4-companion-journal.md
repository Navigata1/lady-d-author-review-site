# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## March Week 4: Love in the Timing of Struggle

Use these pages beside Days 081-087.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, name what I cannot name, call me back to presence, teach me to remember provision, keep speaking after relief, guard obedience from fear, show me mercy in ordinary continuities, and receive my honest Sabbath questions with patience. Amen.

### Day 081 Reflection

**Focus:** God naming pressure before worry defines it.

**Write:** What pressure needs God's naming before I let worry define it?

**Practice:** Ask God to name one pressure clearly before you respond to it.

### Day 082 Reflection

**Focus:** Being called by name in the middle of pressure.

**Write:** Where do I need to let God call me by name before I keep moving?

**Practice:** Pause before one demanding task and answer God honestly: "Here I am."

### Day 083 Reflection

**Focus:** Renaming hard places by God's provision.

**Write:** What hard place might God be inviting me to rename by His provision?

**Practice:** Name one hard place by what God revealed there instead of only by what hurt there.

### Day 084 Reflection

**Focus:** Listening after relief comes.

**Write:** Where has God brought relief, and what might He still be saying there?

**Practice:** After one answered prayer or relief, sit quietly and ask what God wants to form next.

### Day 085 Reflection

**Focus:** Obedience under grace rather than fear.

**Write:** What beloved concern do I need to entrust to God without letting fear define obedience?

**Practice:** Name one beloved concern and surrender your control of it without surrendering God's character.

### Day 086 Reflection

**Focus:** Mercy carried through ordinary continuity.

**Write:** What quiet continuity might be carrying more mercy than I have noticed?

**Practice:** Thank God for one ordinary thread of mercy that has carried you over time.

### Day 087 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath patience for honest questions.

**Write:** What visible facts and missing provision do I need to bring into the Father's presence?

**Practice:** Bring one "where is the provision?" question to God without rushing to answer it yourself.

### Week 4 Review

1. What pressure did God help me name?
2. Where did I hear God call me back to presence?
3. What hard place can now carry a testimony of provision?
4. What did God keep saying after relief came?
5. Where did grace correct fear-based obedience?
6. What ordinary continuity revealed mercy?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest help me hold an honest question?

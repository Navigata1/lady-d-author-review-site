# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## April Week 2: Mercy and Forgiveness

Use these pages beside Days 095-101.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, rename me with mercy, let grace come before performance, awaken humble truth, teach me to pray from Your promise, make love stronger than fear, turn compassion into faithful action, and let Saturday Sabbath peace carry what is vulnerable. Amen.

### Day 095 Reflection

**Focus:** Identity renamed after struggle.

**Write:** What old name needs to lose authority because God's mercy has spoken something truer?

**Practice:** Write one old name fear has used for you, then answer it with a truer identity God is forming.

### Day 096 Reflection

**Focus:** Grace before performance.

**Write:** Where am I trying to perform for peace before receiving the grace God has already given?

**Practice:** Before trying to fix an outcome, pause and receive the mercy that has preserved your life.

### Day 097 Reflection

**Focus:** Humility sent ahead of fear.

**Write:** What honest word of humility may need to go ahead of me?

**Practice:** Draft one humble sentence you could speak, write, or pray before a difficult relational step.

### Day 098 Reflection

**Focus:** Praying from promise.

**Write:** What promise do I need to pray back before I face the next mercy step?

**Practice:** Begin one hard matter by praying back what God has already promised about His presence and care.

### Day 099 Reflection

**Focus:** Love stronger than fear.

**Write:** Where might God be inviting me to stop withholding hope?

**Practice:** Name one guarded place where love may be stronger than the fear that has protected it.

### Day 100 Reflection

**Focus:** Compassion with hands.

**Write:** If mercy had hands today, what faithful action would it take?

**Practice:** Choose one practical act of compassion that makes mercy visible without violating wisdom.

### Day 101 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath-paced restoration.

**Write:** What vulnerable place needs to be carried gently instead of hurried?

**Practice:** Practice one Sabbath-paced act of mercy: slow down enough to notice who or what needs to be carried gently.

### Week Review

1. What old name lost authority this week?
2. Where did grace come before performance?
3. What humble word helped fear loosen its grip?
4. What promise became a prayer?
5. Where did love prove stronger than guarded fear?
6. What practical compassion became visible?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath peace teach me to carry vulnerability gently?

# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## July Week 1: Rain Down on Me

Use these pages with Days 182-188.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, rain on dry places with humble discernment, renewed ordinary faithfulness, freedom from excess, Sabbath breath, remembered mercy, holy movement, and courage to come forward. Keep every lesson submitted to Jesus and useful for love. Amen.

### Day 182 Reflection

Where might I be calling surprise a threat?

Practice: Pause before correcting what unsettles you, and ask God for discernment without rivalry.

### Day 183 Reflection

Where should spiritual insight become ordinary faithfulness today?

Practice: Choose one ordinary place where today's insight must become visible kindness or steadiness.

### Day 184 Reflection

What abundance would still leave my heart unhealed?

Practice: Name one place where desire needs surrender more than it needs more supply.

### Day 185 Reflection

What appetite has been breathing louder than God?

Practice: On this Sabbath, set one appetite before God in prayer before acting on it, and ask whether it leads to life.

### Day 186 Reflection

What painful lesson needs to become wisdom instead of shame?

Practice: Name one lesson from a painful appetite or choice, then ask God to turn it into wisdom.

### Day 187 Reflection

Where have I remained camped at a memory God is calling me to move beyond?

Practice: Identify one corrected area where the next faithful step is movement, not endless replay.

### Day 188 Reflection

What overlooked need needs to be named before God?

Practice: Write one overlooked need plainly before God, then ask for wisdom about the next faithful way to bring it into the light.

### Weekly Review

- What surprise needed discernment rather than rivalry?
- Where did ordinary faithfulness become the place of formation?
- What desire needed surrender more than supply?
- How did Sabbath rest restore breath?
- What warning became wisdom?
- What corrected place needs movement?
- What overlooked need did God invite into the light?

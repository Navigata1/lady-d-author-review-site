# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## September/October Transition: Serving with His Compassion to The Cross and Daily Grace

Use these pages beside Days 270-276.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, trusted Lord and risen Friend, carry me from compassionate service into cross-shaped courage. Give me welcome for the road, light for repentance, grace for witness, freedom to move forward, courage when structures shake, hope that stays on Your way, and Sabbath rest that keeps my heart faithful. Amen.

### Day 270 Reflection

- Who may be waiting on the road for a gentle sign of Christ's welcome?
- Practice: Look for one person outside your usual circle and offer a concrete mercy.

### Day 271 Reflection

- What truth is Christ revealing so that compassion can become whole again?
- Practice: Name one pattern Jesus is exposing, then choose one repentant action.

### Day 272 Reflection

- Where is Jesus inviting my life to witness to the kingdom today?
- Practice: Let one conversation or action quietly witness to the grace of Christ.

### Day 273 Reflection

- What am I tempted to return to when Christ is calling me forward?
- Practice: Identify one backward pull and choose one obedient step away from it.

### Day 274 Reflection

- What have I treated as immovable that Jesus is teaching me to hold loosely?
- Practice: Name one visible security and surrender its authority over your peace.

### Day 275 Reflection

- What urgent voice has been trying to pull me off the road with Jesus?
- Practice: Pause before reacting to one urgent voice, and ask whether it keeps you on the way of Jesus.

### Sabbath Reflection - Day 276

- What spiritual noise do I need to lay down so I can hear Jesus clearly?
- Practice: On this Sabbath, choose quiet worship over spiritual noise and renew your allegiance to Jesus.

### Week Review

- Where did Christ's invitation move my compassion beyond familiar rooms?
- What light helped me repent without shame?
- How did grace become witness this week?
- What backward pull did Jesus help me release?
- Where did Sabbath rest guard my allegiance to the risen Friend?

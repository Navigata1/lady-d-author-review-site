# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## April Week 4: Mercy and Forgiveness

Use these pages beside Days 109-115.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, meet me in honest grief, give sorrow a faithful place and time, make Your love stronger than my fear of being seen, walk with me on the return road, quiet old guilt that expects punishment, teach my words to carry mercy and truth, and root my practical care in Saturday Sabbath rest. Amen.

### Day 109 Reflection

**Focus:** Honest grief in the Father's presence.

**Write:** Where do I need to let grief tell the truth in the Father's presence?

**Practice:** Name one grief honestly before God without apologizing for needing comfort.

### Day 110 Reflection

**Focus:** Patient, time-bound lament.

**Write:** What grief needs a faithful place and time instead of being rushed or ignored?

**Practice:** Give one sorrow a specific place, time, or prayer instead of letting it leak into every room.

### Day 111 Reflection

**Focus:** Visible grief without fear.

**Write:** What grief or need have I been hiding because fear told me to disappear?

**Practice:** Let one trusted person or one honest prayer witness what you have been trying to hide.

### Day 112 Reflection

**Focus:** Returning to ordinary life with compassion.

**Write:** Where do I need restoring compassion as I return to ordinary responsibilities?

**Practice:** Choose one ordinary responsibility to approach gently instead of harshly.

### Day 113 Reflection

**Focus:** Trusting mercy after old guilt speaks.

**Write:** Where am I afraid that mercy was only temporary?

**Practice:** Identify one place where guilt is expecting punishment, and answer it with a truthful reminder of grace.

### Day 114 Reflection

**Focus:** Answering fear with mercy and truth.

**Write:** Where do I need to answer fear with both clarity and gentleness?

**Practice:** Respond to one awkward or fearful communication with both clarity and gentleness.

### Day 115 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath-rooted practical care.

**Write:** What act of care needs to begin from rest instead of pressure?

**Practice:** Before one necessary act of service, pause long enough to receive the Father's love first.

### Week Review

1. What grief did I name honestly before God?
2. Where did patient lament give sorrow a faithful place?
3. What became less hidden because love was stronger than fear?
4. How did compassion shape my return to ordinary responsibilities?
5. Where did I answer old guilt with grace?
6. What communication needed both clarity and gentleness?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest protect practical care from becoming pressure?

# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## March Week 1: The Way in Ordinary Days

Use these pages with Days 060-066.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, walk with me in ordinary obligations, public questions, holy overwhelm, hidden ambition, rescue-shaped attention, gentle welcome, and Sabbath rest from self-managed righteousness. Let the way of ordinary days become a place where Your nearness forms me. Amen.

### Day 060 Reflection

What ordinary obligation needs the nearness and peace of Jesus today?

Practice: Bring one practical responsibility to Jesus before handling it, asking for peace, wisdom, and a clean spirit.

### Day 061 Reflection

Where do I need Jesus to teach me how to think before I act?

Practice: Before responding to one pressure, pause and ask, "What is Jesus teaching me to see here?"

### Day 062 Reflection

Where am I trying to control a holy moment instead of receiving it with Jesus?

Practice: When you feel pressure to manage a meaningful moment, pause for one minute and simply receive it with Jesus.

### Day 063 Reflection

Where is the desire to be seen, ranked, or recognized shaping my ordinary choices?

Practice: Do one quiet act of service today without announcing it or measuring whether anyone noticed.

### Day 064 Reflection

Who might Jesus be asking me to notice, protect, or pray for today?

Practice: Notice one person you might usually overlook, and offer one small act of prayer, kindness, or protection.

### Day 065 Reflection

Where has hurry made me less welcoming to someone vulnerable or dependent?

Practice: Offer patient attention to one person who needs gentleness, especially if the timing feels inconvenient.

### Day 066 Sabbath Reflection

Where am I trying to prove my worth instead of receiving Jesus' grace and following His call?

Practice: On this Sabbath, name one place you are trying to prove your goodness, then release it to Jesus in prayer.

### Week Review

- What ordinary responsibility became more peaceful with Jesus?
- What pressure did Jesus help me think through before reacting?
- Where did I receive a holy moment instead of managing it?
- What quiet service helped ambition loosen its grip?
- Who became more visible through Jesus' rescue-shaped attention?
- Where did gentleness interrupt hurry?
- How did Sabbath rest loosen self-managed righteousness?

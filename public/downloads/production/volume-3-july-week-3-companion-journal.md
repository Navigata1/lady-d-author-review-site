# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## July Week 3: Rain Down on Me

Use these pages with Days 196-202.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, rain on dry places with settled freedom, faithful responsibility, peace in complexity, Sabbath sight, tenderness in transition, accountable mercy, and prayerful care. Keep my limits, legacy, and next steps submitted to Jesus. Amen.

### Day 196 Reflection

Where have I treated structure as the enemy of grace?

Practice: Identify one practical order or responsibility that could become a channel of care instead of a burden.

### Day 197 Reflection

Where does future care need one practical step of faith?

Practice: Take one small practical step that helps future care become clearer.

### Day 198 Reflection

What complicated responsibility needs the Comforter's wisdom?

Practice: Name one complicated responsibility and ask the Comforter for wisdom before reacting.

### Day 199 Reflection

Where is God inviting me to see and bless what I cannot control?

Practice: On this Sabbath, bless one good thing God is doing, even if you cannot control or complete all of it yourself.

### Day 200 Reflection

What limit or transition needs honest tenderness before God?

Practice: Name one limit or transition honestly before God, then ask what faithful tenderness looks like today.

### Day 201 Reflection

Where do I need to receive correction as part of honoring God's holiness?

Practice: Receive one truthful correction without deflecting, and ask God how to honor Him more fully.

### Day 202 Reflection

Who needs my prayerful care in the middle of this transition?

Practice: Pray for the people affected by one transition, not only for your own feelings about it.

### Weekly Review

- What practical order became a channel of care?
- What future responsibility grew clearer?
- Where did the Comforter bring peace to complexity?
- How did Sabbath rest loosen the need to control the future?
- What transition needed tenderness?
- What correction deepened reverence?
- Who did grace teach me to pray for?

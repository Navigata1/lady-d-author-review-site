# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## May Week 3: Fruit That Grows in Surrender

Use these pages with Days 135-141.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, water the places words cannot reach, make God's voice clear in my waiting, breathe willingness into my offering, gather my freedom into holy purpose, strengthen me without hardening me, renew what I have hidden, and anchor my gifts under God's command. Amen.

### Day 135 Reflection

Where have I been pushing without being watered by God?

Practice: Sit quietly before God for three minutes without performing your prayer. Let your breath become surrender.

### Day 136 Reflection

Where do I need God's command more than another opinion?

Practice: On this Sabbath, pause before one decision and ask God to let His command carry more weight than pressure or opinion.

### Day 137 Reflection

Where has fear made my obedience tight-fisted?

Practice: Offer one concrete gift to God today: time, attention, generosity, service, apology, or prayer.

### Day 138 Reflection

Where has my freedom become scattered instead of set apart?

Practice: Choose one freedom you have been spending loosely and dedicate it to God's purpose today.

### Day 139 Reflection

Where am I protecting myself without receiving God's covering?

Practice: Name one place where you need God's covering, then stop defending it in your own strength for today.

### Day 140 Reflection

What precious thing in me needs to be brought back to God?

Practice: Offer God one gift, tenderness, or capacity you have hidden because it felt unsafe or unimportant.

### Day 141 Reflection

Where does my skill or passion need to come under God's command again?

Practice: Before using one gift today, dedicate it to God's command and ask Him to keep it humble.

### Weekly Review

- Where did prayer move beyond performance?
- What did God's command clarify in the waiting?
- What became willing again?
- How did freedom become more set apart?
- Where did God's covering replace self-protection?
- What hidden gift or tenderness returned to God?
- What skill, passion, or fire became more anchored?

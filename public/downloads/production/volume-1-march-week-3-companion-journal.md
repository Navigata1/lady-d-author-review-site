# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## March Week 3: Love in the Timing of Struggle

Use these pages beside Days 074-080.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, lead me beyond false security, correct fear-managed truth with mercy, teach me to worship in transition, receive my impossible questions, hold me by Your covenant promise, bring wise order to my pressures, and form Sabbath rhythm in my heart. Amen.

### Day 074 Reflection

**Focus:** Following God beyond familiar security.

**Write:** What familiar security might I be treating as safer than God's leading?

**Practice:** Hold one familiar security more loosely and ask God for the next faithful step.

### Day 075 Reflection

**Focus:** Correction without shame.

**Write:** Where is the Father patiently inviting me out of fear-managed truth?

**Practice:** Choose one honest repair step instead of defending what fear has managed.

### Day 076 Reflection

**Focus:** Worship in transition.

**Write:** Where can I build a quiet altar of prayer in a season that still feels unsettled?

**Practice:** Turn one temporary place or unfinished situation into a place of prayer.

### Day 077 Reflection

**Focus:** Honest questions under restoring compassion.

**Write:** What promise or hope feels too late, too large, or too impossible for me to carry easily?

**Practice:** Bring the impossible-feeling question to God without polishing it first.

### Day 078 Reflection

**Focus:** God's covenant word over fear-based identity.

**Write:** What fear-based sentence about myself needs to be answered by God's faithfulness?

**Practice:** Replace one "as for me" fear sentence with one truth about God's covenant faithfulness.

### Day 079 Reflection

**Focus:** Peace through God-given order.

**Write:** What pressure needs a God-given name or boundary before I try to carry it?

**Practice:** List three pressures and discern which one God is actually giving you to carry today.

### Day 080 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath rhythm and trust with time.

**Write:** What Sabbath boundary would help me trust God with time instead of trying to control it?

**Practice:** Mark one Saturday Sabbath boundary as an act of trust in God's authority over time.

### Week 3 Review

1. Where did I loosen my grip on familiar security?
2. What honest repair step did correction invite?
3. Where did worship become possible in transition?
4. What impossible-feeling question did I bring to God?
5. What fear sentence was answered by covenant faithfulness?
6. What boundary brought peace to pressure?
7. How did Sabbath rhythm reshape my relationship with time?

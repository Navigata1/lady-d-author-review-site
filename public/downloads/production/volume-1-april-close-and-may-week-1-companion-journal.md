# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## April Close and May Week 1: Mercy Becoming Action

Use these pages beside Days 116-122.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, speak to my heart where fear still listens, settle mercy into ordinary rhythms, form my voice with humility and courage, anchor my promises in integrity, teach me to receive permission without panic, surround faithful action with wise support, and let Saturday Sabbath rest help me carry only what You assign. Amen.

### Day 116 Reflection

**Focus:** Heart-level comfort.

**Write:** What fear needs heart-level comfort instead of irritation?

**Practice:** Name one fear underneath a relationship or responsibility, then answer it with one truthful provision of grace.

### Day 117 Reflection

**Focus:** Mercy becoming ordinary.

**Write:** Where is God asking me to let mercy become ordinary and repeatable?

**Practice:** Choose one ordinary rhythm that can help mercy become a settled way of living.

### Day 118 Reflection

**Focus:** Humble, clear advocacy.

**Write:** What request needs humility, clarity, and courage?

**Practice:** Write one humble, clear request you need to make instead of carrying the need silently.

### Day 119 Reflection

**Focus:** Honorable follow-through.

**Write:** What promise or responsibility needs honorable follow-through?

**Practice:** Identify one promise, responsibility, or commitment that needs honorable follow-through.

### Day 120 Reflection

**Focus:** Permission without panic.

**Write:** Where has God made room for obedience without frantic striving?

**Practice:** Receive one clear permission from God without adding unnecessary striving to it.

### Day 121 Reflection

**Focus:** Love moving with support.

**Write:** What action needs faithful company instead of lonely striving?

**Practice:** Name one faithful action that should not be carried alone, and identify who could walk with you.

### Day 122 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath-rooted wise trust.

**Write:** What needs to move forward, and what needs to be entrusted to God?

**Practice:** Name one action to take and one concern to entrust to God's care.

### Week Review

1. What fear received heart-level comfort this week?
2. Where did mercy become more ordinary and repeatable?
3. What request became clearer and humbler?
4. What promise or responsibility needs honorable follow-through?
5. Where did I receive permission without adding panic?
6. Who could walk with me in faithful action?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest help me know what to carry and what to entrust?

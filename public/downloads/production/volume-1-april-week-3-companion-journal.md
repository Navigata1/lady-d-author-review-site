# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## April Week 3: Mercy and Forgiveness

Use these pages beside Days 102-108.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, make mercy practical for the road, send me with humble truth, be patient with my stunned heart, let words and visible care agree, help identity stand without revenge, teach me to see Your providence without denying harm, and root my prompt obedience in Saturday Sabbath rest. Amen.

### Day 102 Reflection

**Focus:** Provision for the journey.

**Write:** What provision might mercy need for the road ahead?

**Practice:** Name one practical support that could help mercy become easier to receive or practice.

### Day 103 Reflection

**Focus:** Carrying truth home.

**Write:** What truth may God be asking me to carry home with humility?

**Practice:** Identify one place where you need to carry truth, not avoidance.

### Day 104 Reflection

**Focus:** Hope arriving slowly.

**Write:** Where do I need to let hope arrive slowly instead of forcing a reaction?

**Practice:** Give one stunned place permission to receive hope slowly and truthfully.

### Day 105 Reflection

**Focus:** Words and visible care.

**Write:** What visible evidence of care is God asking me to notice or offer?

**Practice:** Match one word of mercy with one visible action of care.

### Day 106 Reflection

**Focus:** Identity without revenge.

**Write:** What truth needs to stand in the room without becoming a weapon?

**Practice:** Name one truth about who you are or what happened, and ask God to hold it in mercy.

### Day 107 Reflection

**Focus:** Providence without denial.

**Write:** Where can I name both the harm and God's sustaining work?

**Practice:** Write one sentence that names harm honestly and one sentence that names God's sustaining work.

### Day 108 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath rest and prompt obedience.

**Write:** What step needs prompt obedience without frantic striving?

**Practice:** Take one prompt mercy step from a rested heart, not a frantic one.

### Week Review

1. What practical provision helped mercy travel this week?
2. Where did I carry truth instead of avoidance?
3. What stunned place received hope slowly?
4. Where did my visible care match my words?
5. What identity truth stood without revenge?
6. How did God help me name harm and providence truthfully?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest protect obedience from becoming frantic?

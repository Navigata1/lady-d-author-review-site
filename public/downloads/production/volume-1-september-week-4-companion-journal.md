# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## September Week 4: Promises That Do Not Fail

Use these pages with Days 266-272.

### Week Prayer

Father, hold me under promises that do not fail. Steady me when difficult reports arrive, meet my fear with covenant mercy, welcome the survival plans that still need healing, awaken beloved identity on Sabbath, teach me to pray specific fears, help me remember grace before performance, and turn prayer into prepared mercy. Amen.

### Day 266 Reflection

What difficult report needs to be held under what God has already revealed?

Practice: Before reacting to one difficult update, name what God has already revealed and let that truth frame your response.

### Day 267 Reflection

Where am I dividing my life into camps because fear expects loss?

Practice: Name one protective plan you are making, then ask God whether it is being led by wisdom, fear, or both.

### Day 268 Reflection

What defensive plan needs the Father's welcome and formation?

Practice: Bring one defensive plan to God and ask Him to keep the wisdom while healing the fear beneath it.

### Day 269 Sabbath Reflection

Where do I need to receive God's increase as mercy instead of measuring it as achievement?

Practice: On this Sabbath, name one place where God has carried you from less to more, then receive it as mercy rather than achievement.

### Day 270 Reflection

What specific fear needs to be named directly before God?

Practice: Pray one specific fear by name before God, including the relationship, outcome, or responsibility attached to it.

### Day 271 Reflection

What task needs to begin from God's promise instead of pressure?

Practice: Write one promise or revealed truth from God above one task you are tempted to approach with pressure.

### Day 272 Reflection

What concrete gesture of mercy needs to be prepared after prayer?

Practice: Prepare one humble, concrete gesture of mercy or repair without using it to control the other person's response.

### Week Review

- What report did I bring under what God had already revealed?
- Where did covenant mercy meet fear without letting fear lead?
- What defensive plan became available for God's formation?
- How did Sabbath rest turn achievement into gratitude?
- What specific fear did I name before God?
- What promise helped me begin from grace instead of pressure?
- What prepared act of mercy became possible after prayer?

# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## May Week 4: Love That Moves Into Action

Use these pages beside Days 137-143.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, make my love practical enough to restore breath, give me courage for clean clarity, return me to covenant mercy when a door closes, make surrender concrete without performance, anchor my identity under You as Creator, find me in unfinished places, and let Saturday Sabbath grace speak light before I try to work. Amen.

---

### Day 137 Reflection

**Focus:** Embodied care that restores life.

**Write:** Where might God be asking my love to become practical enough to help someone breathe?

**Practice:** Choose one embodied act of care that can help someone breathe today: a call, a visit, a meal, a prayer, a ride, or a quiet service.

### Day 138 Reflection

**Focus:** Clean clarity as love.

**Write:** What matter needs clean, loving clarity instead of more delay?

**Practice:** Identify one unresolved matter and take one clean step toward honest clarity: ask, answer, document, schedule, or seek wise counsel.

### Day 139 Reflection

**Focus:** Mercy after a closed door.

**Write:** Where do I need to stop forcing a false redeemer and return to God's covenant mercy?

**Practice:** Release one person or outcome you have been trying to force, and ask God to show the faithful path that remains.

### Day 140 Reflection

**Focus:** Concrete surrender.

**Write:** What concrete step would confirm the surrender I keep leaving vague?

**Practice:** Choose one concrete sign of obedience that confirms your surrender without turning it into display.

### Day 141 Reflection

**Focus:** Created identity under God.

**Write:** What burden becomes lighter when I remember that God is Creator and I am not?

**Practice:** Before starting your main work, pause and confess one sentence: Father, You are Creator, and I am Your beloved creature.

### Day 142 Reflection

**Focus:** God's presence over unfinished places.

**Write:** What unfinished place do I need to stop hiding from God's hovering presence?

**Practice:** Name one unformed place before God and ask the Spirit to hover there with presence, patience, and creative power.

### Day 143 Reflection

**Focus:** Saturday Sabbath light before performance.

**Write:** Where do I need God's light before I try to work, explain, or fix?

**Practice:** Begin Sabbath rest by asking God to speak light into one dark or confused place before you try to solve it.

---

### Week Review

1. Where did my love become embodied care?
2. What became clearer because I handled it in the open?
3. What door closed, and where did I see mercy still making a path?
4. What surrender became concrete instead of vague?
5. How did remembering God as Creator relieve false pressure?
6. Where did I invite the Spirit into an unfinished place?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest help me receive light before performance?

## Day 244 - September 1

### Listen for the Comforter's Nearness

**Scripture Reference:** Judges 6:6

**Production lens correction:** The architecture assigned `esh`; the production lens uses Israel's deep impoverishment and cry to the Lord. The Comforter's nearness is framed as holy mercy that draws a depleted people into honest prayer.

Judges 6:6 names deep need. The people are brought low, and the cry finally turns toward the Lord.

Power to witness does not begin with pretending to be strong. It begins when the heart becomes honest enough to cry out. The Comforter's nearness is not sentimental here; it is holy mercy that comes close to need without flattering the patterns that produced it.

Listen for the Comforter's nearness by asking where depletion has made you quiet, ashamed, or resigned. The Spirit can turn need into prayer before need becomes identity. He can meet the low place and begin forming a witness that tells the truth.

Take one surrendered step before worry gets the first word. Let honest need become prayer today.

The Spirit gives power to witness by drawing depleted hearts into honest prayer.

**Today step:** Name one place of need plainly before God, without dressing it up.

**Prayer:** Holy Spirit, come near in my need. Turn depletion into honest prayer and prepare a truthful witness in me. Amen.

**Journal prompt:** Where do I need to cry out honestly instead of pretending?

**Morning impact:** Take one surrendered step before worry gets the first word.

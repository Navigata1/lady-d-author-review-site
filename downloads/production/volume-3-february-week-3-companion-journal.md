# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## February Week 3: Filled for Daily Life

Use these pages with Days 046-052.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, fill empty places, change the atmosphere of my words, comfort my overwhelmed burdens, provide shared strength, rest on Your people, use ordinary places, and make my heart generous when Your life increases in others. Amen.

### Day 046 Reflection

What needs to fill the space where an old pattern used to live?

Practice: Replace one old pattern with one Spirit-filled practice today: prayer, truth, service, worship, repair, or gratitude.

### Day 047 Reflection

What complaint has been shaping the air around me?

Practice: Catch one complaint before it spreads, and turn it into a prayer or a specific request for help.

### Day 048 Reflection

What burden have I been carrying alone that needs honest prayer and wise help?

Practice: Name one burden that has become too heavy, and ask God for one wise form of help or shared carrying.

### Day 049 Reflection

Where have I confused isolated carrying with faithfulness?

Practice: Invite one wise, appropriate person into a burden you have been trying to carry alone.

### Day 050 Reflection

Where can I carry rain, and where do I need to receive it?

Practice: Offer or receive one concrete act of Spirit-shaped help today.

### Day 051 Reflection

Where have I assumed my ordinary place is too hidden for the Spirit's work?

Practice: Obey one Spirit-prompted action in the ordinary place where you actually are.

### Day 052 Sabbath Reflection

Whose gift do I need to bless instead of compare against my own?

Practice: On this Sabbath, bless one person's Spirit-given gift instead of comparing it to your own.

### Week Review

- What empty place began to be filled?
- How did my words change the atmosphere?
- What burden did I bring into honest prayer?
- Where did I receive shared strength?
- How did I participate in refreshing someone else?
- What ordinary place became holy?
- What comparison did Sabbath rest help me release?

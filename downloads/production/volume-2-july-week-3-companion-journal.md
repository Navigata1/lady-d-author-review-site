# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## July Week 3: The Way, the Truth, and the Life

Use these pages beside Days 193-199.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, teach me childlike courage, truthful forgiveness, mercy beyond the ledger, honest confession, surrendered dependence, received compassion, and Sabbath peace that lays striving down. Let Your way become practical in my family, work, grief, ministry, and private prayer. Amen.

### Day 193 Reflection

- Where is Jesus inviting me to receive childlike dependence instead of performing strength?
- Practice: Choose one moment to listen, learn, or ask for help instead of performing strength.

### Day 194 Reflection

- What forgiveness question do I need to bring honestly to Jesus?
- Practice: Name one forgiveness question before Jesus and ask Him for the next faithful step.

### Day 195 Reflection

- Where has keeping score become a substitute for trusting Jesus?
- Practice: Ask Jesus to show one place where the ledger of resentment is hardening your heart.

### Day 196 Reflection

- What truth do I need to stop hiding so mercy can meet it?
- Practice: Tell Jesus one truth you have been tempted to manage, minimize, or hide.

### Day 197 Reflection

- What need do I need to bring to Jesus plainly, without dressing it up?
- Practice: Pray one plain sentence of need without editing it to sound stronger.

### Day 198 Reflection

- Where have I received mercy that should now shape how I treat someone else?
- Practice: Thank Jesus for mercy you could not earn, then extend one merciful response.

### Sabbath Reflection - Day 199

- What striving is Jesus inviting me to lay down so I can receive His peace like a child?
- Practice: On this Sabbath, release one form of striving and receive care from God without bargaining.

### Week Review

- Where did childlike trust become more practical?
- What did Jesus reveal about forgiveness, mercy, or resentment?
- How did honest need become prayer?
- What did Sabbath rest teach me about laying striving down?

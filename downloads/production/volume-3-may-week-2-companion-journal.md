# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## May Week 2: Fruit That Grows in Surrender

Use these pages with Days 128-134.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, keep me near to the God who sanctifies, give me reverent strength, renew dry places, form faithful rhythm, refine my witness through surrendered limits, set apart my words, and bring me back to gathered listening. Amen.

### Day 128 Reflection

Where am I trying to prove what only God can sanctify?

Practice: Release one performance that is trying to prove holiness, and receive God's sanctifying care in prayer.

### Day 129 Reflection

Where has my obedience become casual around something God calls holy?

Practice: On this Sabbath, honor one holy boundary or practice with quiet reverence instead of casual delay.

### Day 130 Reflection

Where has productivity been naming me before God does?

Practice: Pause one productivity-driven impulse and receive your worth from God before continuing the task.

### Day 131 Reflection

What faithful rhythm needs my next surrendered yes?

Practice: Choose one God-given rhythm and practice it today without waiting to feel impressive.

### Day 132 Reflection

Where has exhaustion become part of my witness?

Practice: Choose one limit you need to honor so your life witnesses to trust rather than anxiety.

### Day 133 Reflection

Where do my words need to carry God's truth instead of my reaction?

Practice: Before speaking into one situation, pause and ask whether your words carry God's truth with humility.

### Day 134 Reflection

Where have I withdrawn from gathered listening under God's word?

Practice: Reach toward faithful community in one concrete way: listen, attend, pray, ask, encourage, or return.

### Weekly Review

- What performance did I release?
- Where did reverence strengthen obedience?
- What stopped naming me when I received worth from God?
- What rhythm became more alive through surrender?
- What limit did I honor as witness?
- How did my words become more surrendered?
- Where did I return to gathered listening?

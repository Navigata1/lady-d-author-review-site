# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## May Week 4: Fruit That Grows in Surrender

Use these pages with Days 142-148.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, give shape to dry places, set me apart near mercy, nourish Your fruit in me, renew hope beneath my witness, bring hidden places into cleansed belonging, help my wordless heart come closer, and anchor me in hidden faithfulness. Amen.

### Day 142 Reflection

Where does my surrender need a holy structure instead of another vague intention?

Practice: Choose one simple structure that will help your dry place receive God today.

### Day 143 Reflection

Where have I confused usefulness with nearness to God?

Practice: On this Sabbath, let one act of service wait long enough for your heart to receive mercy before it moves.

### Day 144 Reflection

Where am I measuring fruit by intensity instead of steady nourishment?

Practice: Receive one ordinary nourishment from God today before trying to prove your growth.

### Day 145 Reflection

Where has my witness become strained because hope needs renewal?

Practice: Before speaking or posting, ask God to renew the hope beneath your witness.

### Day 146 Reflection

Where have I separated my identity in God from honest surrender?

Practice: Offer one hidden or withheld place back to God in honest prayer.

### Day 147 Reflection

Where am I lingering near God but hesitating to come honestly closer?

Practice: Take one quiet step toward God in the place where words feel thin.

### Day 148 Reflection

What needs to be anchored before the next pressure pulls at me?

Practice: Name one anchor God has given you, and return to it before reacting to pressure.

### Weekly Review

- What holy structure helped a dry place receive God?
- How did mercy matter more than usefulness?
- What ordinary nourishment strengthened fruit?
- Where did renewed hope clarify witness?
- What hidden place returned to cleansed belonging?
- What wordless prayer became a step toward God?
- What anchor held steady in pressure?

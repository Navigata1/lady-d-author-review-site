# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## September Week 3: Promises That Do Not Fail

Use these pages with Days 259-265.

### Week Prayer

Father, meet me in the promise that does not fail. Teach me to yield before I perform, mark mercy in the morning, bring honest need into Your patience, rest in Sabbath grace, receive compassion for hard roads, let hope take humble steps, and carry a surrendered heart into my words. Amen.

### Day 259 Reflection

Where am I tempted to rush into performance instead of reverently receiving God's nearness?

Practice: Before beginning one important task, pause and acknowledge God's nearness before asking what you should do next.

### Day 260 Reflection

What mercy from God needs to be marked before the day pulls my attention away?

Practice: Create one small marker of mercy today: a written line, a prayer, a surrendered decision, or an act of obedience tied to what God has shown you.

### Day 261 Reflection

What need do I need to bring honestly to the Father instead of hiding behind religious language?

Practice: Name one real need before God without dressing it up, then place it under what He has already revealed about His care.

### Day 262 Sabbath Reflection

Where has fear made my devotion tense, and how can Sabbath rest return me to grace?

Practice: On this Sabbath, release one anxious attempt to earn security and offer one act of devotion as a response to grace.

### Day 263 Reflection

What difficult relational road needs God's compassion before I try to manage the outcome?

Practice: Before entering one difficult relational space, ask God to make you steady, humble, and receptive to His restoring compassion.

### Day 264 Reflection

What unresolved matter is asking for one wise step rather than another day of worry?

Practice: Take one wise, humble step toward an unresolved matter without trying to control the entire response.

### Day 265 Reflection

Where does my tone need to become more surrendered before I try to explain myself?

Practice: Before one important conversation, ask God to lower pride from your tone while preserving truth and courage.

### Week Review

- Where did I receive God's nearness before trying to perform?
- What mercy did I mark so it would not be swallowed by the day?
- What honest need did I bring to the Father's patience?
- How did Sabbath rest loosen fear from my devotion?
- Where did God's compassion meet me before a difficult relational road?
- What humble step allowed hope to rise?
- How did grace shape my tone, timing, or speech?

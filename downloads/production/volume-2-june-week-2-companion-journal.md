# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## June Week 2: Peace in the Storm

Use these pages beside Days 158-164.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, keep me near after You help me, steady me under scrutiny, teach the source of my words, lead me in truth with tenderness, meet my slow understanding with mercy, anchor Your voice in my heart, and make this Sabbath a gift of holy rest instead of performance pressure. Amen.

### Day 158 Reflection

- Where do I need to remain with Jesus after receiving His help?
- Practice: After one moment of help or relief, pause and remain with Jesus before rushing to the next demand.

### Day 159 Reflection

- Where has criticism been trying to become the atmosphere I breathe?
- Practice: Notice one critical voice and answer it with the steady compassion of Christ.

### Day 160 Reflection

- What has been coming out of my mouth that reveals a deeper place Jesus wants to heal?
- Practice: Before one important conversation, ask Jesus to teach the source of your words.

### Day 161 Reflection

- Where do I need discernment between holy conviction and people-pleasing fear?
- Practice: Review one tense conversation and ask whether you were faithful, careless, fearful, or proud.

### Day 162 Reflection

- Where have I mistaken slow learning for failure?
- Practice: Admit one place where you need Jesus to teach you again, and approach Him without shame.

### Day 163 Reflection

- What pressure most quickly reveals the source of my words?
- Practice: Before responding under pressure, pause and ask, "Jesus, what is happening in my heart?"

### Sabbath Reflection - Day 164

- Where have I confused human pressure with faithful obedience?
- Practice: Release one performance pressure and receive Sabbath rest as a gift from God.

### Week Review

- Where did I stay near Jesus after receiving help?
- What criticism lost its authority over my atmosphere?
- How did Jesus teach the source of my words?
- How did Sabbath rest separate God's gift from performance pressure?

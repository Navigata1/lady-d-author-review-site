# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## June Week 4: Restored by the Father's Heart

Use these pages beside Days 172-178.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, awaken my heart to Your call, sustain me where obedience is difficult, teach me to remember covenant mercy, restore ordinary faithfulness, let peace lead my identity, find my fear before it becomes a plan, and let Saturday Sabbath bring grace before performance. Amen.

---

### Day 172 Reflection

**Focus:** Obedience with real responsibilities.

**Write:** What real responsibility do I need to carry under the Father's guidance instead of using it as a reason to delay obedience?

**Practice:** Name one responsibility you are carrying into obedience, and ask the Father how to steward it without letting it silence His call.

### Day 173 Reflection

**Focus:** Being sent into difficult ground.

**Write:** Where have I mistaken difficult terrain for the absence of the Father's love?

**Practice:** Identify one difficult part of your current place of obedience, and pray for courage to stay faithful without demanding instant ease.

### Day 174 Reflection

**Focus:** Covenant mercy and remembrance.

**Write:** What place in my story needs to become remembrance instead of only unresolved ache?

**Practice:** Write one sentence of remembrance: "The Father met me when..." Then turn it into a short prayer of worship.

### Day 175 Reflection

**Focus:** Ordinary steps under the Father's welcome.

**Write:** What ordinary step of obedience have I been tempted to dismiss because it does not feel dramatic?

**Practice:** Choose one ordinary act of obedience and do it slowly, prayerfully, and without needing it to look impressive.

### Day 176 Reflection

**Focus:** Beloved identity under scarcity pressure.

**Write:** Where is scarcity trying to make me forget that I am still beloved by the Father?

**Practice:** Before making one pressure-filled decision, pause and say aloud: "Need is real, but the Father still names me beloved."

### Day 177 Reflection

**Focus:** Letting love interpret vulnerability.

**Write:** What true concern am I allowing fear to interpret without the Father's love?

**Practice:** Name one vulnerable truth you are facing, then ask the Father to help you interpret it through love instead of fear.

### Day 178 Reflection

**Focus:** Saturday Sabbath and grace before performance.

**Write:** What feared future do I need to bring into Sabbath rest before fear turns it into performance?

**Practice:** Keep a Sabbath pause around one feared outcome. Write the fear honestly, then write one truthful response that does not require manipulation or self-saving performance.

---

### Week Review

1. What responsibility did I carry under the Father's guidance?
2. Where did I stay faithful on difficult ground?
3. What moment became remembrance instead of unresolved ache?
4. Which ordinary step helped me keep walking in welcome?
5. How did peace guard my identity under scarcity pressure?
6. Where did love reinterpret a vulnerable truth?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath interrupt fear's performance and restore grace?

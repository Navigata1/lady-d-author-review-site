# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## November Week 2: Eternal Hope in Grief, Mercy, and Redeemed Intention

Use these pages with Days 308-314.

### Week Prayer

Father, bring eternal hope into grief, memory, obedience, Sabbath rest, old guilt, surrendered authority, and redeemed intention. Teach me to mourn honestly, receive and offer compassion, follow through with love, rest my grief in Your faithfulness, stand where fear trained me to bow, refuse revenge, and trust that You can redeem meaning without denying pain. Amen.

### Day 308 Reflection

Where do I need God's peace to make room for honest grief without letting grief name my future?

Practice: Give one honest grief to God without rushing it, explaining it away, or pretending it is smaller than it is.

### Day 309 Reflection

Where is God inviting me to offer or receive steady presence instead of isolated strength?

Practice: Offer or receive one concrete act of presence around grief without trying to manage the whole story.

### Day 310 Reflection

What faithful step would show that I am leaning into the promise that holds?

Practice: Identify one promise, responsibility, or instruction God is asking you to honor, then take one clear faithful step.

### Day 311 Sabbath Reflection

What grief needs Sabbath rest instead of another round of analysis?

Practice: On this Sabbath, bring one grief into quiet prayer and ask God to place it inside His faithful care.

### Day 312 Reflection

Where is old guilt still teaching me a posture that God's mercy is ready to change?

Practice: Notice one place where fear is making you approach God or others as though mercy has already failed, then pray from beloved identity instead.

### Day 313 Reflection

Where am I tempted to sit in God's chair instead of trusting His authority?

Practice: Name one verdict you have been trying to control, then surrender it to God while keeping any wise boundary He has shown you.

### Day 314 Reflection

What truth about harm and what truth about God's redeeming purpose do I need to hold together today?

Practice: Write one honest sentence about what harmed you and one faithful sentence about what you are entrusting to God's redeeming purpose.

### Week Review

- Where did I allow grief to be honest without letting it name my future?
- What restoring compassion did I offer or receive?
- Which promise or responsibility did I honor with faithful follow-through?
- How did Sabbath rest help me place grief inside God's faithful care?
- Where did mercy begin changing a fear-shaped posture?
- What false authority or verdict did I surrender to God?
- How did eternal hope help me tell the truth about harm while trusting God's redeeming purpose?

# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## September Close / October Week 1: Power to Witness into The Spirit and Prayer

Use these pages with Days 272-278.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, form power to witness through humble fruit and obedient peace, then teach prayer through cleansed desire, attentive speech, Sabbath surrender, trust in hidden providence, and strength under unexpected pressure. Keep every desire, word, and response submitted to Jesus. Amen.

### Day 272 Reflection

How has God's patience met me, and what fruit should it produce today?

Practice: Remember one way God has met your weakness, then let that mercy shape one interaction today.

### Day 273 Reflection

What reassurance has God already given that now calls for obedience?

Practice: Thank God for one reassurance He has already given, then take the next obedient step.

### Day 274 Reflection

What desire needs to be brought home to God before it becomes a decision?

Practice: Bring one desire to God plainly and ask for clean motives before making a decision.

### Day 275 Reflection

Which voice has been loudest in the way I speak about what I want?

Practice: Before repeating a desire out loud, pause and ask what voice is shaping it.

### Day 276 Reflection

Where am I insisting when God may be asking me to listen?

Practice: On this Sabbath, pause one insistence and ask God whether wise counsel has been trying to protect you.

### Day 277 Reflection

Where do I need to trust God's hidden work while still surrendering my motives?

Practice: Name one situation you do not fully understand, then submit your motives to God again.

### Day 278 Reflection

Where do I need God-given breath for unexpected pressure?

Practice: When pressure surprises you, stop for one prayerful breath before responding.

### Weekly Review

- What fruit grew from remembered mercy?
- Where did peace become obedient movement?
- What desire did I bring home to God?
- Which voice shaped my words this week?
- How did Sabbath rest quiet insistence?
- What hidden providence did I trust without excusing my motives?
- Where did prayer become breath under pressure?

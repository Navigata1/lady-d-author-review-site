# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## March Week 2: The Comforter Near You

Use these pages with Days 067-073.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, keep me listening, free, grounded in the Creator, hopeful in the light, clear in discernment, settled in holy rhythm, and spacious in Sabbath rest. Let Your nearness form obedience that looks like Jesus. Amen.

### Day 067 Reflection

What word from God have I resisted because it felt uncomfortable?

Practice: Identify one truth you have been avoiding, and take one small obedient step toward it today.

### Day 068 Reflection

What has been louder than the Spirit's witness in my life lately?

Practice: Turn down one source of noise today and give that attention to prayerful listening.

### Day 069 Reflection

Where am I acting as if everything begins with me?

Practice: Name one burden you have been carrying as if you were the source, and surrender it back to the Creator.

### Day 070 Reflection

Where do I need light for the next step instead of control over the whole path?

Practice: Ask God for light for one next step, then take that step without demanding the entire map.

### Day 071 Reflection

Where have I mistaken confusion for peace?

Practice: Name one place where God is asking you to separate from darkness and choose what is good.

### Day 072 Reflection

Where do I need to receive God's order instead of living the whole future at once?

Practice: Choose one simple rhythm for today: a prayer at morning, a pause at evening, or a surrendered boundary around hurry.

### Day 073 Sabbath Reflection

Where do I need God to create space so I can breathe, listen, and obey?

Practice: On this Sabbath, create one quiet space for prayer and ask God to reorder what pressure has crowded.

### Week Review

- What correction became oil for the journey?
- What noise became easier to turn down?
- Where did I surrender the burden of being the source?
- What next step became clearer in God's light?
- What good thing did the Spirit help me honor?
- What rhythm brought refreshment to a dry place?
- How did Sabbath space help my heart breathe?

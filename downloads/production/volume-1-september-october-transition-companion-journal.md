# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## September/October Transition: Promises That Do Not Fail to Called by Love

Use these pages with Days 273-279.

### Week Prayer

Father, carry me from promises that do not fail into the love that calls me by name. Teach me concrete peace, restoring compassion, patient hope, Sabbath-shaped speech, merciful communication, steady covenant mercy, and a secure heart under Your welcome. Amen.

### Day 273 Reflection

Where does peace need to become a concrete act instead of a vague desire?

Practice: Choose one concrete act of peace that expresses humility without trying to control the other person's response.

### Day 274 Reflection

What detail has love been asking me to notice before I move forward?

Practice: Ask God to show you one detail you have been rushing past, then let that truth shape your next act of care.

### Day 275 Reflection

Where does hope need space, sequence, and restraint instead of urgency?

Practice: Break one pressured situation into the next faithful step, then leave space for God to work before forcing the whole outcome.

### Day 276 Sabbath Reflection

What answer needs to be formed by worship instead of fear?

Practice: On this Sabbath, ask God to prepare one honest answer you may need to give, then rest from rehearsing it in fear.

### Day 277 Reflection

What message is my tone sending ahead of me?

Practice: Send or prepare one message that names truth with humility, honor, and no hidden demand for control.

### Day 278 Reflection

Where does mercy need consistency across more than one moment?

Practice: Choose one repeated interaction where you will practice the same mercy, clarity, and humility more than once.

### Day 279 Reflection

Where do I need the Father's welcome to steady my desire to be received?

Practice: Name one place where you long to be received, then anchor your worth in the Father's welcome before you take the next step.

### Week Review

- Where did peace become concrete without trying to control an outcome?
- What detail did restoring compassion ask me to notice?
- How did hope become patient, ordered, and practical?
- How did Sabbath rest shape my words before pressure arrived?
- What message did mercy send ahead of me?
- Where did covenant mercy become steady through repetition?
- How did the Father's welcome steady my longing to be received?

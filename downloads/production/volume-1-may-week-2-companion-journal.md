# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## May Week 2: Love That Moves Into Action

Use these pages beside Days 123-129.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, bring my hidden counsel into Your compassion, teach me to find my good in You, name me by Your strength before the day does, send me by covenant mercy instead of anxiety, hear my distress before fear becomes my plan, cover me as I act from refuge, and let Saturday Sabbath rest form confidence and reverence in me. Amen.

---

### Day 123 Reflection

**Focus:** Inner counsel becoming prayer.

**Write:** What inner conversation needs to become prayer instead of private exhaustion?

**Practice:** Write one hidden question you have been carrying, then pray it plainly instead of rehearsing it alone.

### Day 124 Reflection

**Focus:** Goodness held in God.

**Write:** Where am I trying to build peace apart from God's presence?

**Practice:** Name one place where you have been trying to secure goodness apart from God, and surrender it back to His lordship.

### Day 125 Reflection

**Focus:** Strength naming identity.

**Write:** Where has God been strength in a place I almost only remember as struggle?

**Practice:** Say aloud one place where God has been your strength, then let that truth guide your next action.

### Day 126 Reflection

**Focus:** Small action inside long mercy.

**Write:** What small action may belong to a larger mercy than I can measure?

**Practice:** Take one action that fits God's long faithfulness, even if you cannot see the full outcome yet.

### Day 127 Reflection

**Focus:** Distress becoming a call.

**Write:** What distress needs to become a call to God before it becomes a decision?

**Practice:** Before acting from distress, pause long enough to call on God by name.

### Day 128 Reflection

**Focus:** Acting from refuge.

**Write:** Where am I acting exposed when the Father is inviting me under His covering?

**Practice:** Identify one pressure where you need to act from refuge instead of exposure.

### Day 129 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath mercy and reverence.

**Write:** How does mercy help me come near, and how does reverence teach me to bow?

**Practice:** Enter Sabbath rest by naming one mercy that gives you confidence and one way reverence should shape your response.

---

### Week Review

1. What hidden counsel became prayer this week?
2. Where did I remember that my good is not apart from God?
3. How did God's strength name me before pressure did?
4. What action did I take from covenant mercy instead of anxiety?
5. Where did I call on God before distress became my plan?
6. Where did I act from refuge instead of exposure?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest deepen beloved identity, mercy, and reverence?

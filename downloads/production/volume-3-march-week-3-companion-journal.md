# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## March Week 3: The Comforter Near You

Use these pages with Days 074-080.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, bring loving order, grounded witness, gathered belonging, wordless prayer, breath in waiting, peace with timing, and Sabbath freedom into this week. Let me walk in God's light with a surrendered heart. Amen.

### Day 074 Reflection

Where does love need to become holy order in me?

Practice: Name one tangled area of your life and ask God what loving boundary or order is needed there.

### Day 075 Reflection

Where am I trying to name myself instead of receiving my place from God?

Practice: Name one ordinary place God has given you, and ask how you can witness there without performing importance.

### Day 076 Reflection

Where do I need God to gather me and give me a place to stand?

Practice: Write down three scattered concerns, then pray over them as one surrendered offering to God.

### Day 077 Reflection

What am I trying too hard to explain that God is simply asking me to bring?

Practice: Sit quietly before God for two minutes with one concern you cannot explain, and trust Him to hold it.

### Day 078 Reflection

What unfinished matter do I need to release into God's timing today?

Practice: Choose one unfinished matter to release at evening prayer instead of carrying it through the night.

### Day 079 Reflection

What season am I in, and what faithful action belongs to this day?

Practice: Name the actual season you are in, and choose one faithful action that fits this day.

### Day 080 Sabbath Reflection

What light has God already given me, and what peaceful response belongs to it?

Practice: On this Sabbath, name one light God has given and choose one peaceful response to it.

### Week Review

- What loving order did the Spirit begin forming?
- Where did God free me from proving my place?
- What scattered concern did God gather?
- What wordless prayer helped me trust?
- What did I release into God's timing?
- What season did I name more honestly?
- How did Sabbath light lead to freedom?

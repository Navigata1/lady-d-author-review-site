# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## October Week 4: The Cross and Daily Grace

Use these pages beside Days 291-297.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, risen Shepherd and abiding Lord, awaken joy in faithfulness, correct fear's picture of God, tell me the truth with compassion, move received grace into living service, make mercy visible on the road, heal striving through Your welcome, and let Sabbath nearness become practical compassion. Amen.

### Day 291 Reflection

- Where has faithfulness begun to feel joyless, and what would it mean to serve with Jesus there?
- Practice: Do one faithful task from joy with Jesus rather than pressure to prove yourself.

### Day 292 Reflection

- Where has fear quietly taught me to see God as harsh instead of good?
- Practice: Name one fear-shaped belief about God, then answer it with one truth revealed in Jesus.

### Day 293 Reflection

- What excuse has started to sound reasonable because I have repeated it too long?
- Practice: Identify one excuse that has protected delay, and replace it with one faithful action.

### Day 294 Reflection

- What received grace needs to move instead of remain unused?
- Practice: Put one entrusted resource, skill, or opportunity into motion for love.

### Day 295 Reflection

- Where is grace asking to become visible instead of remaining only a feeling?
- Practice: Make one act of mercy concrete enough that someone can receive it.

### Day 296 Reflection

- Where do I need to receive God's welcome before I try to serve well?
- Practice: Serve someone today from gratitude for grace, not from pressure to earn God's approval.

### Sabbath Reflection - Day 297

- Who needs my presence, prayer, or practical care without performance or pride?
- Practice: On this Sabbath, pray for one person in embodied need and choose one concrete way to honor their dignity.

### Week Review

- Where did joy return to faithful work?
- What distorted picture of God did Jesus correct?
- What excuse gave way to action?
- How did grace become visible mercy?
- How did Sabbath nearness restore practical compassion?

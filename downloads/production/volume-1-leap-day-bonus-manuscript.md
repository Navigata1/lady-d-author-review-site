## Bonus / Leap Day - February 29

### Grace for the Extra Day

**Scripture Reference:** Exodus 33:12

**Production lens correction:** The architecture assigned `hesed`; the production lens uses `yada/shem/chen/am` - know, name, favor, and people - because the verse centers Moses' appeal that God knows him by name, has shown favor, and has entrusted him with a people.

Grace for the extra day begins with being known. Exodus 33:12 stands in the middle of a tender conversation between Moses and the Lord. Moses carries responsibility he did not invent. He is leading people who are not easy to lead. He is aware that calling without God's presence would be too heavy.

Beloved identity needs this kind of honesty. Sometimes an extra day can feel like a gift. Sometimes it can feel like one more stretch of responsibility. One more morning of questions. One more place where you need clarity, patience, provision, or courage. Moses does not pretend the assignment is light. He brings the weight of it before God.

The comfort in this verse is not that Moses has everything figured out. The comfort is that God knows him by name. Grace does not begin with perfect management. Grace begins with the Father's knowing. You are not merely a role, a helper, a parent, a worker, a minister, a survivor, or the person everyone expects to be strong. You are known.

This leap-day bonus is an invitation to receive time as mercy. The extra day is not another stage for proving yourself. It is another place to ask the Father to lead what you cannot carry alone. His favor is not shallow applause. It is the nearness of God meeting a real person with a real assignment.

Grace for the extra day means you can pause before you push. You can ask for the Lord's presence before you accept the weight of the next responsibility. You can remember that the God who calls also knows your name.

**Today step:** Name one responsibility you have been carrying as if you were unknown, then ask the Father to meet you there by name.

**Prayer:** Father, thank You for knowing me by name. Meet me in the responsibilities that feel too heavy to carry without You. Let this extra day become a place of grace, presence, and beloved identity. Amen.

**Journal prompt:** Where do I need to receive the Father's knowing before I carry the next responsibility?

**Morning impact:** Let the Father's love carry Grace for the Extra Day into one faithful step today.

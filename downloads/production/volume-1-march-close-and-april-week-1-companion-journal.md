# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## March Close and April Week 1: Love in the Timing of Struggle into Mercy and Forgiveness

Use these pages beside Days 088-094.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, refine my love through wise boundaries, meet me on the road, help me rename hard places by Your presence, send me into concrete mercy, move my listening into obedience, let conviction become clarity instead of shame, and strengthen beloved identity through Saturday Sabbath rest. Amen.

### Day 088 Reflection

**Focus:** Boundaries that protect promise.

**Write:** What boundary may be protecting God's promise in me rather than punishing me?

**Practice:** Identify one choice where love is asking for a clearer boundary.

### Day 089 Reflection

**Focus:** Restoring compassion on the road.

**Write:** Where do I need to hear God's promise while I am still on the road?

**Practice:** Name one place where you feel away from home and ask God to meet you there.

### Day 090 Reflection

**Focus:** Renaming a place by God's presence.

**Write:** What place in my story needs a truer name because God met me there?

**Practice:** Write one old name for a hard place, then write a truer name shaped by God's presence.

### Day 091 Reflection

**Focus:** Concrete mercy instead of vague intention.

**Write:** What is one faithful mercy step I can take without pretending everything is already healed?

**Practice:** Choose one concrete mercy step that is faithful, honest, and safe.

### Day 092 Reflection

**Focus:** Listening that becomes movement.

**Write:** What have I already heard clearly that now needs movement?

**Practice:** Turn one mercy insight into one visible action before the day ends.

### Day 093 Reflection

**Focus:** Conviction without shame.

**Write:** What pattern is God helping me see clearly enough to change?

**Practice:** Name one pattern you can now see more clearly, and choose one wiser response.

### Day 094 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath rest in beloved identity.

**Write:** Where do I need to remember that God's company is with me on the road of mercy?

**Practice:** Mark one Saturday Sabbath pause as a reminder that God is with you on the road of mercy.

### Week Review

1. What boundary protected promise this week?
2. Where did God meet me on the road?
3. What hard place received a truer name?
4. What concrete mercy step became possible?
5. What listening turned into movement?
6. What pattern did conviction help me see without shame?
7. How did Saturday Sabbath rest strengthen beloved identity?

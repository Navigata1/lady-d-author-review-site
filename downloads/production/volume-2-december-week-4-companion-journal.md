# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## December Week 4: Walking as His Witness

Use these pages beside Days 356-362.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, undivided Lord and saving Truth, gather my divided places, heal my judgment, show Yourself stronger than what binds, teach me mercy-shaped interpretation, gather my scattered heart in Sabbath rest, keep me tender to the Spirit, and form visible fruit from a renewed root. Amen.

### Day 356 Reflection

- Where is my witness weakened by divided allegiance?
- Practice: Identify one divided allegiance and choose one concrete action that agrees with Jesus.

### Day 357 Reflection

- Where do I judge with uneven measures?
- Practice: Ask Jesus to reveal one place where you are using uneven measures, then practice humility there.

### Day 358 Reflection

- What has felt stronger than me that needs to be brought under Christ's stronger authority?
- Practice: Name one binding fear, habit, or lie and confess Jesus as stronger over it.

### Day 359 Reflection

- Where do I need Jesus to correct how I interpret human need?
- Practice: When you see a need today, pause before judging and ask Jesus for Scripture-shaped mercy.

### Sabbath Reflection - Day 360

- What scattered part of my life needs to be gathered back to Jesus this Sabbath?
- Practice: On this Sabbath, name one scattered part of your life and invite Jesus to gather it back under His allegiance.

### Day 361 Reflection

- Where is the Spirit inviting me to respond with surrender instead of delay?
- Practice: Respond to one clear conviction with repentance, repair, or obedience instead of delay.

### Day 362 Reflection

- What fruit in my life reveals a root Jesus wants to heal?
- Practice: Bring one recurring fruit problem to Jesus and ask what root He wants to heal or form.

### Week Review

- Where did Jesus gather divided allegiance?
- What uneven measure did He correct?
- How did Christ's stronger authority change my peace?
- How did Sabbath rest gather my scattered heart?
- What root-level formation is beginning to bear fruit?

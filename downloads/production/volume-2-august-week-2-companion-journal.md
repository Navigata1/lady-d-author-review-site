# Walking with Jesus - Companion Journal

## August Week 2: The Surrendered Heart

Use these pages beside Days 214-220.

### Week Prayer

Jesus, Prince of Peace and trusted Lord, teach my surrendered heart to rest in what God can do, bring honest questions into Your presence, receive courage for costly obedience, handle hard covenant questions with humility, seek healing instead of loopholes, become tender where pain has hardened me, and let this Sabbath form truth and mercy in my life. Amen.

### Day 214 Reflection

- Where have I treated impossibility as the final word?
- Practice: Name one impossible place and take one small obedient step that agrees with God's nearness.

### Day 215 Reflection

- What question about surrender do I need to bring plainly to Jesus?
- Practice: Tell Jesus one honest question about the cost of following Him.

### Day 216 Reflection

- Where does costly obedience need courage instead of resentment?
- Practice: Name one costly obedience and ask Jesus to give courage without bitterness.

### Day 217 Reflection

- Where do I need to follow Jesus in a difficult question instead of trying to control the answer?
- Practice: Before one difficult conversation, ask Jesus to remove the desire to trap, win, or wound.

### Day 218 Reflection

- Where might I be asking for permission to stay hard instead of asking Jesus to heal me?
- Practice: Ask Jesus to show one place where pain is asking for permission instead of healing.

### Day 219 Reflection

- Where has my heart become guarded, sharp, or resistant?
- Practice: Practice one act of tenderness where your heart has become guarded.

### Sabbath Reflection - Day 220

- Where do truth, mercy, and surrender need to meet in my covenant life?
- Practice: On this Sabbath, ask Jesus where truth and mercy need to govern a covenant place in your heart.

### Week Review

- Where did impossibility become an invitation to trust God?
- What honest question did I bring to Jesus?
- How did Jesus give courage for costly obedience?
- Where did truth soften a hardened place?
- How did Sabbath rest quiet self-defense and make room for faithful mercy?

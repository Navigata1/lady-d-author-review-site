# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## November Week 2: Freedom, Fire, and Holiness

Use these pages with Days 314-320.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, form freedom, fire, and holiness in ordinary returns, simple answers, restrained witness, public attention, remembered mercy, cleansed desire, and exposed identity. Keep me steady after spiritual moments, honest in ordinary speech, wise with unfinished work, peaceful in visible responsibility, fruitful in Sabbath memory, surrendered in desire, and sealed in belonging. Amen.

### Day 314 Reflection

How do I return to ordinary life after a meaningful encounter with God?

Practice: After a meaningful moment, choose one ordinary faithful action instead of chasing intensity.

### Day 315 Reflection

How do I answer ordinary questions when something deeper is happening in me?

Practice: Give one ordinary answer today with humility and no spiritual exaggeration.

### Day 316 Reflection

Where do I share too much because silence feels vulnerable?

Practice: Protect one unfinished work of God by refusing to over-explain it.

### Day 317 Reflection

Where does public attention unsettle my obedience?

Practice: Before a public or visible responsibility, pause and remember you stand before God first.

### Day 318 Reflection

What deliverance am I forgetting because present fear feels louder?

Practice: On this Sabbath, write down one way God has delivered or sustained you.

### Day 319 Reflection

Where do I want something visible to make faith feel safer?

Practice: Name one visible security you are tempted to trust more than God.

### Day 320 Reflection

Where does attention make me feel exposed instead of held?

Practice: When attention narrows toward you, breathe and remember that God holds you before people see you.

### Weekly Review

- What ordinary faithful action followed a meaningful moment?
- Where did an answer stay humble and honest?
- What unfinished work remained wisely covered?
- How did public attention bow before God's presence?
- How did Sabbath memory become fruit?
- What desire for control was cleansed?
- Where did belonging steady exposed identity?

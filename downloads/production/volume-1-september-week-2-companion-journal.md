# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## September Week 2: Promises That Do Not Fail

Use these pages with Days 252-258.

### Week Prayer

Father, meet me on the road of promises that do not fail. Search my motives, form obedience in transition, give me rest in ordinary places, protect Sabbath mercy, widen my welcome into blessing, and awaken me to Your nearness. Amen.

### Day 252 Reflection

Where am I tempted to fix appearances before surrendering the deeper motive to God?

Practice: Before making one corrective move, pause and ask whether it is rooted in surrender or only in the need to look right.

### Day 253 Reflection

What road is asking me to move with obedience instead of fear?

Practice: Take one faithful next step on a difficult road, and name it as obedience rather than escape.

### Day 254 Reflection

What ordinary or uncomfortable place might God be using as a meeting place?

Practice: Receive one ordinary place of rest today as a possible meeting place with God.

### Day 255 Sabbath Reflection

What ladder of self-proving do I need to release this Sabbath?

Practice: On this Sabbath, release one ladder of self-proving and receive God's mercy as the first movement toward you.

### Day 256 Reflection

Where is the Father inviting me to breathe wider than private survival?

Practice: Name one way God's welcome can become blessing for someone beyond your private need today.

### Day 257 Reflection

What uncertainty needs to be answered first by the promise, "I am with you"?

Practice: Speak one current uncertainty to God, then answer it with the promise of His presence before taking the next step.

### Day 258 Reflection

Where can I now see that God was present even when I did not know it?

Practice: Look back over one hard place and name one sign that God was nearer than you realized.

### Week Review

- Where did God expose a repair attempt that needed deeper surrender?
- What road became a place to practice obedience?
- What ordinary resting place became more open to God's presence?
- How did Sabbath mercy free me from a ladder of self-proving?
- Where did the Father's welcome widen into blessing for someone else?
- What uncertainty was steadied by God's promise of presence?
- Where did I recognize that God had been near before I knew it?

# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## October Week 4: Called by Love

Use these pages with Days 294-300.

### Week Prayer

Father, call me by love through honest timelines, preserving peace, patient comfort, Sabbath provision, restoring compassion, tender grace, and hope that makes speech possible again. Let Your love form truth, mercy, wisdom, provision, tenderness, and patient restoration in me this week. Amen.

### Day 294 Reflection

Where do I need grace to help me tell the truth about a season that is not finished yet?

Practice: Name the truth of one ongoing pressure without exaggerating or minimizing it, then ask God for grace to pace today's obedience.

### Day 295 Reflection

Where have I seen God preserve life or hope in a place that once felt only painful?

Practice: Ask God to show you one place where He has preserved life, faith, wisdom, or tenderness through a hard season.

### Day 296 Reflection

Where do I need to stop demanding instant repair and receive God's patient care?

Practice: Identify one area where you need a protected place for slow restoration, then take one step toward the care God is offering.

### Day 297 Sabbath Reflection

What future need do I need to bring into Sabbath rest before fear writes the plan?

Practice: On this Sabbath, bring one future need before God and ask Him to replace fearful planning with restful, practical trust.

### Day 298 Reflection

Where do my words need to become clearer, tenderer, and more trustworthy?

Practice: Before a meaningful conversation, ask God for words that are honest enough to be clear and tender enough to carry peace.

### Day 299 Reflection

Where has survival tightened tenderness that God wants to restore with wisdom?

Practice: Notice one place where survival has tightened your tenderness, then invite God to soften it without removing wisdom.

### Day 300 Reflection

Where am I trying to force speech before mercy, trust, and truth have prepared the room?

Practice: Ask God whether one strained relationship needs patient prayer, honest words, repaired trust, or a quieter boundary before speech can be restored.

### Week Review

- What ongoing season did I name truthfully before God?
- Where did I see God's preserving work more clearly?
- What protected place for restoration did I begin to receive?
- How did Sabbath rest quiet fear about a future need?
- Where did truthful peace begin shaping my words?
- What tenderness did grace begin restoring?
- Where did hope teach me to speak, wait, repair, or keep a wise boundary?

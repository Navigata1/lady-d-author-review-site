# Filled with the Holy Spirit - Companion Journal

## September Week 4: Power to Witness

Use these pages with Days 265-271.

### Week Prayer

Holy Spirit, form power to witness through courage under opposition, freedom from false defenses, identity under God's word, renewed love under gathered pressure, Sabbath readiness, patient mercy for trembling obedience, and assurance that serves faithful action. Keep every witness submitted to Jesus. Amen.

### Day 265 Reflection

Where has criticism made me question obedience God already clarified?

Practice: Name one place where obedience may be misunderstood, then ask God for courage without bitterness.

### Day 266 Reflection

What have I been defending out of habit, fear, or pressure?

Practice: Release one false defense and choose one honest sentence of truth before God.

### Day 267 Reflection

What label needs to be answered by God's truer word?

Practice: Write one true identity statement before God, then let it answer one false label.

### Day 268 Reflection

Where has pressure made me assume God is far away?

Practice: In one pressured place, pause and ask, "Holy Spirit, how is Jesus near here?"

### Day 269 Reflection

Where has readiness become restless pressure?

Practice: On this Sabbath, pray for one person you may need to encourage, then reach out with calm faith instead of pressure.

### Day 270 Reflection

What anxious question needs to be brought into God's presence?

Practice: Bring one anxious question to God, then obey the part of His instruction you already know.

### Day 271 Reflection

Where am I seeking another sign because the next step feels costly?

Practice: Ask God honestly for help, then take one concrete step tied to what He has already made clear.

### Weekly Review

- Where did God give courage without bitterness?
- What false defense did I release?
- Which label was answered by God's truer word?
- How did I notice Jesus near under pressure?
- How did Sabbath rest purify readiness?
- What anxious question did I bring to God?
- Where did assurance strengthen obedience instead of replacing it?

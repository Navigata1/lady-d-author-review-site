# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## March Week 2: Love in the Timing of Struggle

Use these pages beside Days 067-073.

### Weekly Opening Prayer

Father, come near where I have become stiff, meet me in ordinary faithfulness, give me truth to carry, anchor my hope in Your covenant name, remind me that You see what has been done, slow me enough to turn aside, and teach me Sabbath worship before performance. Amen.

### Day 067 Reflection

**Focus:** Inviting God into the stiff place.

**Write:** Where has pressure made me stiff, and how can I invite the Father into that place honestly?

**Practice:** Confess one hard place without hiding or excusing it, then ask God to come near there.

### Day 068 Reflection

**Focus:** Ordinary faithfulness as preparation.

**Write:** What ordinary responsibility might be a place of preparation rather than a place of delay?

**Practice:** Name one routine task as holy ground and do it with quiet faithfulness.

### Day 069 Reflection

**Focus:** Receiving truth before carrying responsibility.

**Write:** Where am I trying to carry responsibility before receiving what God has revealed?

**Practice:** Before answering a pressure, pause and ask the Father for the true word your heart needs first.

### Day 070 Reflection

**Focus:** Hope rooted in God's faithful name.

**Write:** What part of God's past faithfulness needs to speak into my present struggle?

**Practice:** Remember one earlier mercy and let it shape one decision today.

### Day 071 Reflection

**Focus:** Being seen by the Father.

**Write:** What part of my story needs to be brought under the Father's seeing instead of carried alone?

**Practice:** Write one sentence that begins, "The Father sees..." and keep it visible through the day.

### Day 072 Reflection

**Focus:** Turning aside from survival speed.

**Write:** What holy interruption might I miss if I keep moving at the pace of worry?

**Practice:** Take one unhurried pause and ask what God is calling you to notice.

### Day 073 Reflection

**Focus:** Sabbath worship before performance.

**Write:** Where do I need reverent awe to free me from performing for acceptance?

**Practice:** Take a Saturday Sabbath pause to worship God for who He is before asking what to do next.

### Week 2 Review

1. Where did honest confession soften a stiff place?
2. What ordinary responsibility became a place of preparation?
3. What truth did I need before I carried responsibility?
4. Which memory of God's faithfulness strengthened hope?
5. Where did being seen by the Father change how I carried the week?
6. What did I notice when I turned aside?
7. How did Sabbath worship challenge performance?

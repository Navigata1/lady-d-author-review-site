# Surrendering to God's Love - Companion Journal

## December Week 2: Daily Surrender Through Victory Song and Steady Love

Use these pages with Days 336-342.

### Week Prayer

Father, teach me daily surrender through Your covenant mercy, Your welcome, my beloved identity, Sabbath-rooted peace, grace before performance, mercy in the morning, and Your patient love. Let every step this week begin from what You have revealed instead of what fear demands. Amen.

### Day 336 Reflection

Where do I need to receive God's mercy as protective strength, not only gentle comfort?

Practice: Name one battle you have been carrying alone, then surrender the first anxious thought to the Father before you act.

### Day 337 Reflection

What old message still tries to tell me I do not fully belong to the Father's love?

Practice: Before noon, take one concrete action that matches belonging: pray honestly, answer gently, release false shame, or obey without begging for worth.

### Day 338 Reflection

What demand most often tempts me to forget that I am already loved and redeemed?

Practice: Write one sentence that begins, "Because the Father has redeemed me..." and let that truth guide one decision today.

### Day 339 Sabbath Reflection

What concern needs to rest under God's faithfulness this Sabbath before I try to carry it again?

Practice: On this Sabbath, let one unfinished concern rest under God's faithful strength before you return to action.

### Day 340 Reflection

Where is my heart tempted to perform instead of receiving grace and obeying freely?

Practice: Before one responsibility today, pause long enough to receive grace, then act without using the task to prove your worth.

### Day 341 Reflection

What voice usually tries to interpret my morning before the Father's mercy speaks?

Practice: Before checking the loudest demand of the day, give God the first honest sentence and ask mercy to lead your attention.

### Day 342 Reflection

What outcome am I trying to rush because I am afraid patience means abandonment?

Practice: Choose one delayed outcome and pray over it without forcing the next step beyond what God has given you today.

### Week Review

- Where did covenant mercy become protective strength?
- How did the Father's welcome change one act of obedience?
- What demand lost authority because beloved identity spoke first?
- What concern did Sabbath rest place under God's faithfulness?
- Where did grace interrupt performance?
- What morning voice did mercy correct?
- What delayed outcome did I entrust to the Father's patient love?

Meeting synthesis

Lady D Devotional System — June 14 Meeting Synthesis

Source files: Google Meet transcript, meeting summary notes, attached MP3 audio file (present, 88.48 minutes).

Decisions Captured

1. Primary title changed: the first 365-day devotional is now Surrendering to God’s Love.

2. Three-volume devotional set:

  • Volume 1: Surrendering to God’s Love — God the Father / love, identity, surrender.
  • Volume 2: Walking with Jesus — the Son / discipleship and daily nearness.
  • Volume 3: Filled with the Holy Spirit — Spirit / comfort, filling, fruit, rain, fire, power.

3. Reader-facing date system: month + day labels, not weekdays. Example: January 1, January 2. No Monday/Tuesday labels.

4. February 29 stays: Lady specifically wants 29 days in February so the devotional can flow in leap years. Treat Feb 29 as bonus/leap-day material when publishing a standard 365 edition.

5. Daily manuscript format: each day should become a full readable print page: devotional title, scripture, 4–5 paragraphs with depth, prayer, journal prompt.

6. Typography/accessibility: no tiny fonts. Mature-eye readable layout matters.

7. Voice workflow: Lady should provide voice notes rather than typed edits; Jon translates voice feedback into manuscript revisions.

8. Companion journals: each volume gets an aligned companion journal; journal visual style should match the devotional and avoid generic/overused tropes.

9. 31-day visual system: strongly approved by Lady; she sees it as a preaching/ministry tool and likely bestseller-style event table item.

10. Future testimony lane: Being Covered Through the Storm / Being Carried Through the Storm, with a red umbrella in rain as central image. Capture 5–6 freeform voice recordings and structure later.

11. Publishing lane: Amazon KDP first for print-on-demand and credibility; Gumroad/Etsy/audiobook/Spotify/Audible/direct web later depending on revenue and fit.

12. Business terms discussed: possible $2,000 bundle for three volumes, companion journals, and 31-day system with payment plan; needs formal contract/invoice.

Author Voice Notes

Lady D wants work that is spiritual, alive, and not boxed into stale religious categories. Her voice can be direct, joyful, preacherly, practical, and sometimes funny. She wants depth over thin devotional filler, and she wants the reader to feel as if Lady D is speaking directly to them in the morning.

Production Rule Update

The interface is a talk-to-me workspace, not the final book. The book page itself should be cleaner: month/day label, devotional title, scripture, full-page body, prayer, journal prompt. Interface labels like “Month 1” are acceptable for navigation but should not be treated as final interior copy.
